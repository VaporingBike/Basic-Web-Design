About the Movie

introduction:

How to Train Your Dragon, released in 2010, is an animated adventure film from DreamWorks Animation. Directed by Dean DeBlois and Chris Sanders, the film is set in a Viking village called Berk, where humans and dragons are locked in a battle of fear and survival. The story follows Hiccup Horrendous Haddock III, a young Viking who unexpectedly forms a bond with the very dragon his people seek to destroy—Toothless, the rare Night Fury.

Plot summary:

Hiccup is a misfit Viking trying to prove his worth in a world that values strength and bravery. After capturing a Night Fury dragon, he sets out to learn about dragons in secret. As Hiccup’s bond with Toothless grows, he uncovers a deeper truth: dragons aren't the savage creatures his village believes them to be. With the help of Toothless, Hiccup challenges the village’s long-held beliefs and works to bring peace between dragons and humans, culminating in a daring battle against the monstrous Red Death dragon that threatens both species.

Key Characters

  • Hiccup Horrendous Haddock III: The protagonist, an inventive yet awkward Viking who struggles to meet his father's expectations. Hiccup learns that brains, not just brawn, are key to leadership, and his bond with Toothless becomes his greatest strength.
  • Toothless: The elusive Night Fury dragon. Initially feared for his speed and power, Toothless is revealed to be kind, loyal, and playful, with a deep connection to Hiccup. Their friendship forms the heart of the movie.
  • Stoick the Vast: Hiccup’s father and the chief of Berk. Stoick’s belief in the necessity of fighting dragons challenges Hiccup’s more empathetic approach. As the film progresses, Stoick’s view of dragons—and of his son—evolves.
  • Astrid Hofferson: A brave and determined Viking, Astrid begins as Hiccup’s classmate and competitor. As the story unfolds, she becomes an ally and helps Hiccup face the true danger threatening Berk.
  • Gobber the Belch: The village blacksmith and a mentor to Hiccup, Gobber adds humor and wisdom to the story, teaching Hiccup the art of dragon-fighting while also offering sage advice.

Themes & Messages

  • Friendship & Trust: At its core, the movie is about the transformative power of friendship. Hiccup’s relationship with Toothless teaches that trust and understanding can bridge even the greatest divides.
  • Challenging Traditions: The film encourages questioning traditions that are based on fear or prejudice. Hiccup’s defiance of the Viking way of fighting dragons illustrates that there’s strength in understanding and empathy, not just in violence.
  • Self-Discovery: Hiccup’s growth throughout the film highlights the importance of embracing your unique qualities. His inventiveness and intellect make him an unlikely hero, showing that true leadership comes from embracing who you are.
